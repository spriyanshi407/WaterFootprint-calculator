Understanding Water Footprint

Measuring our impact on the world's water resources

What is Water Footprint?

The water footprint is a measure of humanity's appropriation of fresh water in volumes of water consumed and/or polluted. It was introduced by Dutch professor Arjen Hoekstra in 2002 as a way to measure the total volume of freshwater used to produce the goods and services consumed by individuals, communities, or businesses.

Blue Water Footprint

The volume of surface and groundwater consumed (evaporated or incorporated into a product) as a result of the production of a good or service.

Green Water Footprint

The volume of rainwater consumed during the production process, particularly in agricultural and forestry processes.

Grey Water Footprint

The volume of freshwater required to assimilate pollutants to meet specific water quality standards.

Why is it Important?

Understanding and reducing our water footprint is crucial because:

  • Freshwater is a limited resource, with only 2.5% of Earth's water being fresh
  • Many regions face water scarcity and stress
  • Water pollution affects ecosystems and human health
  • Climate change is altering water availability patterns
  • Population growth increases water demand
