Water Saving Tips

Simple changes that make a big difference

Shorter Showers

Reduce your shower time by just 2 minutes to save up to 20 gallons of water per shower.

Potential Savings: 7,300 gallons/year

Fix Leaks

A leaking toilet can waste up to 200 gallons of water per day. Check for leaks regularly.

Potential Savings: 73,000 gallons/year

Turn Off Tap

Turn off the tap while brushing your teeth or shaving to save up to 4 gallons per minute.

Potential Savings: 2,920 gallons/year

Full Loads Only

Run your dishwasher only when it's full to save water and energy.

Potential Savings: 1,000 gallons/year

Efficient Washing

Use a basin when washing dishes by hand instead of running water continuously.

Potential Savings: 5,000 gallons/year

Drinking Water

Keep a pitcher of water in the fridge instead of running the tap until it's cold.

Potential Savings: 200 gallons/year

Smart Watering

Water your garden in the early morning or late evening to reduce evaporation.

Potential Savings: 1,500 gallons/year

Car Washing

Use a bucket instead of a hose to wash your car, or visit a commercial car wash that recycles water.

Potential Savings: 100 gallons/wash

Native Plants

Plant drought-resistant native plants that require less water to thrive.

Potential Savings: 2,000 gallons/year

Laundry Habits

Wash full loads of laundry and use cold water when possible.

Potential Savings: 3,000 gallons/year

Conscious Consumption

Choose products with lower water footprints and support water-conscious companies.

Varies by product

Spread Awareness

Share water-saving tips with friends and family to multiply your impact.
